Dhondup Wangchen released, thanks supporters

Submitted by bafot on Thu, 2014-06-05 23:36

Phayul[Thursday, June 05, 2014 16:15]

DHARAMSHALA, JUNE 5: The jailed Tibetan filmmaker Dhondup Wangchen has been released this morning after serving six years’ imprisonment sentence, said Gyaljong Tsetrin, Dhondup’s cousin who lives in Switzerland.

He was driven by the police to Khotse, (about two hours drive away from Xining) where he reached his sister's home around 15.00hrs local time.

Zurich, January 21, 2013: In an unusual move, it has been learned by Filming for Tibet that filmmaker and political prisoner Dhondup Wangchen has been transferred from Xichuan labour camp to Qinghai Provincial Women’s Prison, the main prison for women in China’s Qinghai province [1].

Join the Rangzen Walk around the Bay Area March 23--30

Submitted by bafot on Mon, 2013-01-28 11:58

Dear Friends,

Please join with me, Tibetans, and Americans to for a Freedom - Rangzen Walk - any step along the way of 108 miles that will be undertaken to highlight Tibetan rights.

Rangzen: International Tibet Independence Movement will be leading a March for Tibet's Independence in 2013, from March 23 to 30 in the Bay Area. March co-sponsors include Bay Area Friends of Tibet, Tibetan Association of Northern California, San Francisco Students for a Free Tibet, and San Francisco Regional Tibetan Youth Congress.
